🔍 Browser Focus Mode Extension

A Chrome extension that flips the focus blocker model: instead of blocking what distracts you, it defines what you need. Full product arc from discovery interviews through PRD, metrics, roadmap, and Calm Technology principles.

Calm Technology System Design User Research PRD Writing

The Problem

When a user is trying to focus in a particular browser tab, the presence of other open tabs, along with the ability to open new ones, creates a constant source of distraction. The average user has 17 tabs open at any given time, and productivity communities consistently identify tab-switching as a primary focus killer.

Discovery method: Personal experience as a proxy user, validated through five structured user interviews. Core discipline: ask about the past, never the future.

Sample interview questions:

  • "Tell me about an instance where you felt distracted by multiple tabs open in your browser."
  • "Tell me about the last time you had to do focused work. What was the setup you had in place before starting?"
  • "Walk me through a time when, despite all measures you had in place, you still ended up getting distracted."

The User

Primary segment: Students and knowledge workers doing self-directed deep work without a hard external deadline enforcing focus.

Jobs To Be Done: When a student or knowledge worker is trying to focus on a task and is feeling distracted, they want to accomplish the task at hand successfully within a given time, so they can finish what they set out to do and feel their time was genuinely well spent.

Key insight: The real job is not "block distracting websites." It is "create conditions where I can do my best work and feel the satisfaction of finishing what I started."

The Market

Product Core Bet Strength Blind Spot
Cold Turkey Block what distracts you Most aggressive blocker; application-level whitelisting No browser-level tab control; cannot define an intentional workspace
Freedom Cross-platform restriction with scheduling Available on mobile; syncs across devices Same subtractive model; no tab-level workspace control
StayFocusd Lightweight browser-based time limits Free Chrome extension; simple to set up Time limits cap distraction but don't prevent it; no workspace concept
Cold Turkey blocks what you don't want. This extension defines what you do want. That is the difference between building walls and building a room.

The Solution (PRD)

A Chrome extension allowing users to initiate a timed focus session capped at 90 minutes. Once a session begins, the browser enters a locked state: the user cannot switch to any tab outside a pre-defined group of approved tabs. If a user attempts to exit before the timer completes, they see a 15-second countdown to confirm. The goal is pause, not punishment.

Non-goals for v1: No customisable UI · No session lockout · No Pomodoro timer · No cross-device sync · Chrome only

Success Metrics

Metric Definition Rationale
North Star Distinct users completing 2+ focus sessions per week Signals habit formation, not just trial
Guardrail Uninstall rate after sessions Catches long-term failure even when short-term looks healthy
Input (30 days) Users completing 15+ sessions in first month Signal the product has become routine, not novelty

Roadmap

Now: Sprint 1 Next: Sprint 2 & 3 Later: When conditions are right
Tab locking with interval sessions (max 90 min) Tab border highlighting (Calm Tech Principle 5) Motivational quotes on exit, only after user testing
Chained tab groups (approved workspace) Countdown before session starts (Calm Tech Principle 2) Session completion celebration
15-second countdown exit mechanism Peripheral time indicator (Calm Tech Principle 3) Optional to-do list, only if validated

Key Trade-offs

  • Exit friction over hard lockout: 15-second countdown trades absolute enforcement for user trust.
  • Chrome only in v1: Covers ~65% of browser market share. A polished Chrome experience beats a mediocre multi-browser one.
  • No customisation in v1: Personalising a focus app contradicts its core purpose (Calm Technology Principle 1).

📝 The Middle Ground Note-Taking App

A note-taking app for the user every major product ignores: the middle user who needs more than Apple Notes but less than Notion. Competitive analysis, user persona, PRD, and the discipline of resisting feature creep.

User Research PRD Writing Competitive Analysis Market Strategy

The Problem

The mid-20s to early 30s age group (students and working professionals) are being let down by existing note-taking apps. No app is easy enough to use immediately without configuration, yet powerful enough for both short-form capture and long-form collaboration.

Three sources of evidence:

  • Personal experience: As a postgraduate student, the friction of switching between tools and Notion's setup failure were experienced directly.
  • Competitive analysis: Every product either optimised for simplicity and became limiting, or optimised for power and became inaccessible.
  • Behavioural evidence: Users routing around Google Docs for lightweight capture (using Apple Notes or WhatsApp instead) is strong evidence of a product-job mismatch.

The User

User persona: James, 25. A master's student in the US. Collaborates with classmates on group projects and does his own writing. Finds Google Docs good for collaboration but poor for personal work.

Once, James spent 45 minutes customising a Notion template for his job search. He ended up so frustrated that he paid for an AI recruiter instead, because the AI was easier to set up than the template.

Jobs To Be Done: When I need to write, organise, or collaborate on something, I want a tool I can open and use immediately without configuration, so I can focus on the work itself rather than setting up the environment for the work.

The Market

Product Core Bet Strength Blind Spot
Google Docs Documents belong to teams Real-time co-editing, version history Architecturally heavy for solo use; users route around it for casual capture
Apple Notes Frictionless capture above all else Instantly accessible; no account needed Designed for capture, not knowledge management; breaks down at scale
Evernote One tool for everything Cross-platform, powerful search Added too many features until the core promise became diluted
Notion Infinite flexibility Database-as-document; genuinely new Blank canvas is paralysing; users spend more time building systems than doing work
If you are trying to build a product that benefits the user, this is the kind of friction you have to hold out against.

The Solution

A note-taking app enabling collaboration and long/short-form writing without configuration. Open and start writing immediately.

The differentiator: Every feature must earn its place by solving a real user problem, not by responding to competitive pressure.

Success Metrics

Metric Definition Rationale
North Star Distinct users creating 2+ docs (200+ chars) per week OR contributing to a shared doc Captures both solo and collaborative use
Guardrail 14-day inactive rate Rising rate signals onboarding is failing to deliver the frictionless promise
Input (30 days) % of new users completing first doc within 24 hours Tells us immediately if the no-configuration promise is being delivered

Roadmap

Now: Sprint 1 Next: Sprint 2 & 3 Later: When conditions are right
Core writing editor (bold, italic, headings, bullets) Templates: 2 or 3 pre-built structures AI writing assistance, when a capable open source model is ready
Real-time collaboration with link sharing How-to guide on first login Additional integrations, user feedback only, never competitive pressure
Clean minimal UI, no configuration required Opt-in weekly digest of what the user created

Key Trade-offs

  • Resisting competitive iteration: Not iterating in response to competitor releases. Slower feature velocity preserves the core promise long term.
  • Real value over vanity metrics: Documents completed and collaborative contributions over daily active users or time in app.

🎵 Spotify: Discover Music

A product improvement recommendation for Spotify's discovery engine, identifying that the recommendation problem is a data source problem and proposing a community music intelligence layer to surface music niche listeners have never heard but would love.

Competitive Analysis User Research PRD Writing Market Strategy

The Strategic Bet

Spotify's discovery problem is not an algorithm problem. It's a data source problem.

Spotify's recommendation engine is trained on behavioral data: what you play, skip, repeat, and save. It is exceptionally good at finding more of what you already know you like. But for niche listeners, behavioral data has a ceiling. Once you've listened to everything Spotify's algorithm knows you'd like, discovery plateaus.

The missing signal is community music intelligence: the kind of knowledge that lives in Reddit threads, blog posts, YouTube comments, and Discogs reviews. Text-form taste knowledge that behavioral data cannot capture.

The strategic bet: build a layer that reads that signal.

The User

Arjun, 24, niche listener, Spotify Premium subscriber.

  • Listens to jazz fusion, Tamil indie, ambient electronic
  • Discovery has plateaued; Spotify keeps recommending the same 40–50 artists
  • Workarounds: YouTube rabbit holes, Brave browser searches, subreddit spelunking
  • Pain: the workaround works, but it's effortful and disconnected from his Spotify library
  • He doesn't want to leave Spotify. He wants Spotify to do what Reddit does.

Two Jobs to Be Done

Job Description Spotify's Performance
Job 1 Serve me within my known taste Done well
Job 2 Take me somewhere genuinely new Fails structurally

Job 2 is where Spotify bleeds niche users to YouTube Music and Reddit. The gap isn't effort, it's architecture. Behavioral data cannot surface what you haven't heard.

Competitive Landscape

Platform Discovery Mechanism Niche Listener Performance
Spotify Behavioral ML (plays, skips, saves) Plateaus quickly for niche users
YouTube Music Video engagement signals + search intent Better for rabbit holes, worse for library
Apple Music Editorial curation + behavioral Strong editorial, weak personalization depth

The shared gap: All three fail niche listeners for Job 2. None of them read community text signals at scale.

The Gap

Behavioral data is a closed loop: you can only recommend what the user has already signaled interest in. For a mainstream listener, this loop is wide enough. For Arjun, it's exhausted in months.

Community knowledge breaks the loop. When someone on r/jazzmemes writes "if you like Hiatus Kaiyote, you need to hear Moonchild", that's a discovery signal. Spotify doesn't read it. No streaming platform does at scale.

Product Recommendation: Discover Music

A new feature inside the Search tab. The user types or speaks a description of what they want to discover:

  • "Something like Hiatus Kaiyote but more acoustic"
  • "Tamil indie with a lo-fi feel"
  • "Ambient electronic for late-night focus"

Spotify parses the input, maps it to community music knowledge (Reddit, Discogs, Pitchfork, etc.), and returns a curated playlist of tracks the user has never heard.

Community Intelligence Layer: A text-trained model on music community content surfaces associations that behavioral data misses, going beyond tags to actual prose about what music feels like, sounds like, and what it's good for.

Intensity Filter:

Filter Description
Take it easy Adjacent to your known taste
Go crazy Noticeable stretch, still coherent
Music from another planet Genuinely unfamiliar territory

History-Off Mode: Discovery sessions don't feed back into your main recommendation engine unless you explicitly save tracks. This preserves the integrity of your core taste profile.

How This Is Different from DJ

Dimension DJ Feature Discover Music
Input None (passive) Natural language (active)
Data source Your behavioral history Community music intelligence
Discovery depth Bounded by your history Breaks out of your history
History isolation No Yes (history-off mode)

DJ is a better shuffle. Discover Music is a new discovery primitive.

Objection Defenses

  • "Isn't this just the DJ feature?" DJ is reactive: it uses what you already know. Discover Music is proactive: it uses what the community knows. The input mechanism and data source are both different.
  • "Won't users just switch to YouTube Music?" YouTube Music's discovery is better for rabbit holes but disconnected from library management. Discover Music keeps the discovery session inside Spotify's ecosystem, with seamless save-to-library flow.
  • "Discovery Mode already exists for artists. How is this different?" Discovery Mode is an artist-side tool for label promotion, not a listener-side discovery tool. These are entirely different surfaces with different stakeholders.

Success Metrics

  • Discovery session starts per week (niche listener cohort)
  • New artist saves per session (tracks saved from Discover Music that weren't in library before)
  • 7-day retention on discovered tracks
  • Churn rate delta for niche listeners vs. control group
  • NPS delta among Spotify Premium niche listener segment
